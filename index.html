Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah

1876 - 1948

Founder of Pakistan

Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah was the founder of Pakistan. He was born in Karachi, British India, in 1876. He was a lawyer and politician who led the All-India Muslim League to the creation of Pakistan in 1947. He is considered the Father of Pakistan. Jinnah was a brilliant lawyer and a skilled politician. He was also a strong advocate for the rights of Muslims in India. He believed that "Muslims would not be able to achieve their full potential under British rule", and he worked tirelessly to create a separate homeland for Muslims. In 1947, Jinnah's dream was finally realized when Pakistan was created. He became the first Governor-General of Pakistan, and he served in that role until his death in 1948.

Some Important points about Muhammad ALi Jinnah

  • He was educated in India and England.
  • He was a member of the Indian National Congress, but he resigned in 1920 to protest the Congress's decision to support a united India.
  • He was the president of the All-India Muslim League from 1934 to 1947.
  • He was a skilled negotiator, and he played a key role in the negotiations that led to the creation of Pakistan
  • He was a devout Muslim, and he believed that Islam was the basis for Pakistan's identity.
  • His motto was:

    "Unity, Faith and Discipline"

    1. Unity is the ability of the people of Pakistan to come together and work towards a common goal.
    2. Faith is the belief in the future of Pakistan and the strength to overcome challenges.
    3. Discipline is the willingness to follow the rules and regulations that are necessary for a successful nation

    Here are some quotes from Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah that illustrate his belief in these principles:

  • "You are now all Pakistanis, not Balochis, Pathans, Sindhis, Bengalis, Punjabis and so on and as Pakistanis we must feel, behave and act, and we should be proud to be known as Pakistanis and nothing else."
  • "Faith is the mainspring of all actions. Without it, nothing great can be achieved."
  • "Discipline is the soul of an army. It makes an army efficient."
